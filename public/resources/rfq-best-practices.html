RFQ Best Practices Guide

Write effective RFQs that get you better quotes and faster responses

Why RFQ Quality Matters

A well-crafted Request for Quote (RFQ) is the foundation of successful procurement. Clear, detailed RFQs help suppliers understand your needs precisely, resulting in:

  • More accurate pricing (fewer surprises later)
  • Faster response times from suppliers
  • Better ability to compare quotes apples-to-apples
  • Reduced back-and-forth communication
  • Stronger supplier relationships from the start

Essential RFQ Components

  1. Clear Product Specifications

    Be specific about dimensions, materials, colors, and any technical requirements. Vague descriptions lead to inaccurate quotes.

  2. Quantity Requirements

    Include exact quantities needed, and consider requesting tiered pricing for different volumes.

  3. Timeline & Delivery Details

    Specify your required delivery date, shipping destination(s), and any flexibility in timing.

  4. Quality Standards

    Define acceptable quality levels, required certifications, and any testing requirements.

  5. Budget Indication

    While optional, providing a budget range helps suppliers tailor their proposals appropriately.

Quantity Strategies

üí° Pro Tip: Always request tiered pricing. Ask for quotes at multiple quantity levels (e.g., 1,000 / 2,500 / 5,000 units) to understand volume discounts and help with future planning.

Sample Tiered Pricing Request

Include in your RFQ:

  • Initial Order: 2,500 units
  • Please also quote: 1,000, 5,000, and 10,000 units
  • Estimated Annual Volume: 15,000-20,000 units

Timeline Best Practices

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake: Setting unrealistic deadlines frustrates suppliers and limits your options. Standard production times are typically 2-4 weeks plus shipping.

Realistic Timeline Planning

  • Quote Response: Allow 2-5 business days
  • Sample Production: 3-7 business days
  • Sample Shipping: 2-5 business days
  • Bulk Production: 10-20 business days (varies by product)
  • Bulk Shipping: 3-10 business days (domestic)

What to Ask For in Quotes

Request that suppliers include the following in their quotes:

  • Unit price at each quantity level
  • Setup/origination fees
  • Any additional charges (PMS matching, proofs, etc.)
  • Production timeline
  • Shipping cost estimate to your location
  • Payment terms
  • Sample availability and cost
  • Certifications held

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Being too vague - Suppliers need details to quote accurately
  2. Unrealistic timelines - Rush fees add up quickly
  3. Forgetting shipping costs - Can significantly impact total cost
  4. Not requesting samples - Always verify quality before bulk orders
  5. Ignoring MOQs - Minimum order quantities affect pricing
  6. Poor artwork quality - Low-res files delay the process
  7. Not specifying units - Inches vs. centimeters matters!
